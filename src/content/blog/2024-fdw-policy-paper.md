Co-authored with Nayoung Lee, this paper applies critical discourse analysis to South Korea's recent foreign domestic worker policy — arguing that its rhetorical scaffolding reproduces a racialized gender order, framing migrant care work through assumptions that reinscribe both gendered and racial hierarchies in domestic labour.

Published in *Korea Social Policy Review*, 31(3), 181–210.
